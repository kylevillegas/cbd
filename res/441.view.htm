316<h1 id="894b" class="is it cp dw iu iv iw hj ix iy iz hn ja jb jc jd je jf jg jh ji jj jk jl jm jn dm">

317Why don’t more people know about the endocannabinoid system (ECS)?</h1>

318<p id="73ee" class="he hf cp hg b hh jo hj hk hl jp hn ho hp jq hr hs ht jr hv hw hx js hz ia ib ch dm">

319We have known about the ECS for about 25 years. It was first discovered in the 1990&#x27;s, by L.A. Matsuda. Dr. Matsuda was the first to describe the structure and functional expression of the cannabinoid receptor, CB-1. Scientists were trying to understand how <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetrahydrocannabinol" class="eh ic" target="_blank" rel="noopener ugc nofollow">

320THC</a>

321, the primary intoxicating substance in marijuana, affected the body. What they discovered was a remarkably complex network of cannabinoid receptors (CBr) expressed in cells of both the central and peripheral nervous system</p>

322<p id="5b55" class="he hf cp hg b hh hi hj hk hl hm hn ho hp hq hr hs ht hu hv hw hx hy hz ia ib ch dm">

323Since then other cannabinoid receptors have been identified including cannabinoid receptor type 2 (CB-2), which is found primarily in the immune system, digestive system (gastrointestinal-tract), and many of the body’s major organs. But these receptors were only half the story. The discovery of CB-1 and CB-2, prompted a hunt for the body’s own cannabinoid-like chemicals that naturally interact with these newly discovered receptors. Why would the body produce receptors if there were no naturally occurring cannabinoids that would bind with them?</p>

324<p id="1c04" class="he hf cp hg b hh hi hj hk hl hm hn ho hp hq hr hs ht hu hv hw hx hy hz ia ib ch dm">

325The first cannabinoid-like chemicals to be discovered was <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anandamide" class="eh ic" target="_blank" rel="noopener ugc nofollow">

326Anandamide</a>

327. Anandamide acts on both the CB-1 and CB-2 receptors, modulating both the central or peripheral nervous system, respectively. Activity in the peripheral nervous system is in regulating the of functions of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immune_system" class="eh ic" target="_blank" rel="noopener ugc nofollow">

328immune system</a>

329.</p>

330<h1 id="37cf" class="is it cp dw iu iv iw hj ix iy iz hn ja jb jc jd je jf jg jh ji jj jk jl jm jn dm">

331What exactly does the endocannabinoid system (ECS) do?</h1>

332<p id="0eb1" class="he hf cp hg b hh jo hj hk hl jp hn ho hp jq hr hs ht jr hv hw hx js hz ia ib ch dm">

333We do not have a full and complete picture of what the ECS does but we do know that the ECS helps fine-tune most of our vital physiological functions. It promotes homeostasis affecting everything from sleep, appetite, pain, inflammation, memory, mood, and even reproduction. So in basic terms, the ECS helps modulate the regulation of homeostasis across all major body systems ensuring that all systems work in concert with one another.</p>

334<h2 id="ff48" class="jt it cp dw iu ju jv jw ix jx jy jz ja ka kb kc je kd ke kf ji kg kh ki jm kj dm">

3351. All animals have an endocannabinoid system (ECS).</h2>

336<p id="8bc6" class="he hf cp hg b hh jo hj hk hl jp hn ho hp jq hr hs ht jr hv hw hx js hz ia ib ch dm">

337As humans beings, we like to think of ourselves as special, but we’re not special for having an ECS. All vertebrates and invertebrates are known to have an ECS. The most primitive animal found to express cannabinoid receptors is the sea-squirts, an animal which evolved over 600 million years ago.</p>

338<h2 id="8b4e" class="jt it cp dw iu ju jv jw ix jx jy jz ja ka kb kc je kd ke kf ji kg kh ki jm kj dm">

3392. Endocannabinoid receptors are the most abundant neuromodulatory receptors in the body.</h2>

340<p id="a841" class="he hf cp hg b hh jo hj hk hl jp hn ho hp jq hr hs ht jr hv hw hx js hz ia ib ch dm">

341The total number of endocannabinoid receptors in the body is believed to be greater than all other neuromodulatory receptors found in the body combined, including receptors for the neurotransmitters serotonin and dopamine. Anandamide alone has <a href="http://www.jbc.org/content/287/25/20851.full" class="eh ic" target="_blank" rel="noopener ugc nofollow">

342the most receptors in the brain</a>

343 and is critical for maintaining a healthy central nervous system.</p>
