407<h3>

408What is CBD Oil?</h3>

409<p>

410CBD or cannabidiol oil is derived from the cannabis sativa (hemp) plant. Instead of using the seeds, CBD is extracted from the flowers, leaves, stems, and stalks of matured hemp plants. CBD oil can be extracted through several methods, including carbon dioxide, ethanol, and even olive oil. The extraction method can play a part in the quality of CBD oil.</p>

411<h3>

412Similarities Between Hemp Oil and CBD Oil</h3>

413<ul>

414<li>

415Both are oils.</li>

416<li>

417Both can come from the cannabis sativa plant.</li>

418<li>

419Both are non-intoxicating.</li>

420<li>

421Both can be applied topically or ingested.</li>

422</ul>

423<h3>

424</h3>

425<h3>

426Differences Between Hemp Oil and CBD Oil</h3>

427<ul>

428<li>

429Each is derived from a different part of the plant.</li>

430<li>

431Hemp oil can only be derived from the industrial hemp variety of cannabis sativa, CBD oil can be sourced through several different varieties of the cannabis plant.</li>

432<li>

433Reputable CBD oil will come with a <a href="https://nuleafnaturals.com/how-to-read-a-certificate-of-analysis/">

434certificate of analysis</a>

435 (COA) that details exactly what’s in the product. Hemp oil will not come with a COA.</li>

436<li>

437CBD oil can legally contain up to 0.3% THC based on federal law in the U.S. Hemp oil contains no THC. State and local laws may add other regulations to this.</li>

438<li>

439Some states have laws that apply to the distribution and manufacture of CBD; hemp oil containing no THC has no such restrictions.</li>

440<li>

441Hemp oil is a nutrient-rich oil; it contains no cannabinoids and does not reap the benefits of the entourage effect.</li>

442<li>

443Hemp oil can be purchased on <a href="https://nuleafnaturals.com/cbd-oil-amazon/">

444Amazon</a>

445, but CBD cannot.</li>

446<li>

447Hemp oil has been used in beauty products for several years, but CBD is relatively new to all industries.</li>

448<li>

449Though they can both be used topically or ingested, you’re more likely to use hemp oil topically and ingest CBD oil.</li>

450</ul>

451<h3>

452Hemp Oil vs. CBD Oil and the Entourage Effect</h3>

453<h3>

454<img loading="lazy" class="alignleft wp-image-1065470 size-medium" src="https://nuleafnaturals.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Hemp-oil-vs.-full-spectrum-CBD-oil-300x219.png" alt="Hemp oil vs. full spectrum CBD oil" width="300" height="219" srcset="https://nuleafnaturals.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Hemp-oil-vs.-full-spectrum-CBD-oil-300x219.png 300w, https://nuleafnaturals.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Hemp-oil-vs.-full-spectrum-CBD-oil-600x437.png 600w, https://nuleafnaturals.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/Hemp-oil-vs.-full-spectrum-CBD-oil.png 740w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />

455</h3>

456<p style="text-align: left;">

457Hemp oil has many unique benefits but cannot give you the same as CBD oil thanks to the entourage effect. It is manufactured only from the seeds of the cannabis sativa plant, while <a href="https://nuleafnaturals.com/product/full-spectrum-hemp-cbd-oil-60mg-ml/">

458full spectrum CBD oil</a>

459 uses the entire plant from stem to leaf for production.</p>

460<p>

461When you harvest oil from the entire plant, you get every potential beneficial compound the plant has to offer, including not just CBD, but dozens of other cannabinoids and scent compounds called terpenes.</p>

462<p>

463Hemp seed oil doesn’t have the same cannabinoid profile, <a href="https://nuleafnaturals.com/what-are-terpenes-in-cbd-oil/">

464terpenes</a>

465, or other beneficial compounds. You’re only getting a fraction of the benefits that you would from CBD oil and not tapping into the entourage effect.</p>

466<p>

467Why does it matter? <a href="https://nuleafnaturals.com/the-entourage-effect/">

468The entourage effect</a>

469 is the belief that the different compounds of the cannabis plant work better when they’re used together compared to being used individually. The terpenes help the cannabinoids achieve their best effects; the cannabinoids help other cannabinoids – everything works together harmoniously.</p>

470<p>

471With the entourage effect, the whole is greater than the sum of its parts. You get the benefits of the entourage effect with full spectrum CBD oil but not with hemp seed oil.</p>

472<h3>

473Reading Hemp Oil and CBD Oil Labels</h3>

474<p>

475CBD oil is currently unregulated. Marketers and manufacturers might use misleading labeling or claim unfound benefits on their products. Always read the ingredients list to be certain what you’re getting and ignore what the label says.</p>

476<p>

477Hemp oil should always use hemp seed oil as the active ingredient, while CBD oil should list cannabidiol, full spectrum CBD, broad spectrum CBD, hemp (not hempseed), or phytocannabinoid-rich CBD as its primary ingredient.</p>
