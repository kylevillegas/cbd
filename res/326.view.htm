1588</p>

1620<span class="heading-toc" id="uses"></span> <h2 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-10" class="comp mntl-sc-block health-sc-block-heading mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> Uses </span> </h2>

1621<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-11" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1622The most common mental illness in the U.S., anxiety disorders affects more than 18 percent of the population each year, according to the Anxiety and Depression Association of America.

1623</p>

1624<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-12" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1625<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-13" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1626Although anxiety disorders are generally treated with psychotherapy, medication, or a combination of the two, many people opt to forgo these standard approaches and self-treat with products like CBD oil.

1627</p>

1628<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-14" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1629<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-15" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1630According to a survey published in <em>Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research</em> in 2018, almost 62 percent of cannabidiol users reported that they used CBD to treat a medical condition, with the top three conditions being pain, anxiety, and depression.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="1"></span>

1631</p>

1632<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-16" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1633<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-17" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1634Due to a lack of research, scientists aren’t sure how CBD oil might help treat issues like anxiety. Some research suggests that in addition to impacting the endocannabinoid system, cannabidiol may influence receptors involved in the modulation of serotonin (a chemical messenger thought to play a role in anxiety regulation).

1635</p>

1636<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-18" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1637<span class="heading-toc" id="research"></span> <h2 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-19" class="comp mntl-sc-block health-sc-block-heading mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> Research </span> </h2>

1638<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-20" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1639So far, most of the evidence for CBD’s effects on anxiety comes from animal studies and laboratory experiments.

1640</p>

1641<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-21" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1642<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-22" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1643For a report published in the journal <em>Neurotherapeutics</em> in 2015, scientists analyzed this preliminary research and found that CBD oil shows promise in the acute treatment of conditions like generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, obsessive-compulsive disorder, and post-traumatic stress disorder.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="8">﻿</span>﻿

1644</p>

1645<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-23" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1646<h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-25" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-subheading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-subheading__text"> Social Anxiety Study </span> </h3>

1647<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-26" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1648While there’s currently a lack of large-scale clinical trials testing the use of CBD oil in the treatment of anxiety, a small study published in <em>Neuropsychopharmacology</em> in 2011 determined that CBD may help alleviate social anxiety.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="2">﻿</span>﻿

1649</p>

1650<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-27" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1651<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-28" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1652For this study, 24 people with social anxiety disorder received either 600 milligrams (mg) of CBD or a placebo an hour and a half before performing a simulated public speaking test. Additionally, 12 other people with social anxiety disorder performed the same test without receiving any CBD treatment.

1653</p>

1654<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-29" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1655<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-30" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1656Results revealed that pre-treatment with CBD significantly reduced anxiety, cognitive impairment, and discomfort while participants were delivering their speech.

1657</p>

1658<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-31" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1659<h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-33" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-subheading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-subheading__text"> Dose-Response Study </span> </h3>

1660<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-34" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1661The anxiety-reducing effect of CBD may follow a bell-shaped dose-response curve, suggests a study published in <em>Frontiers in Pharmacology</em>.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="3">﻿</span>﻿ After administering different dosages of CBD before a public speaking test, researchers found that subjective anxiety measures were lowered with the 300 mg CBD dose, but not with the 100 or 900 mg CBD dosages.

1662</p>

1663<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-35" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1664<h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-37" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-subheading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-subheading__text"> Paranoid Trait Study </span> </h3>

1665<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-38" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1666Another study, published in the <em>Journal of Psychopharmacology</em> in 2018, tested the effects of cannabidiol in people with high paranoid traits and found that cannabidiol had no impact on anxiety, cortisol levels, heart rate, systolic blood pressure (the top number in a blood pressure reading), and persecutory ideation.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="4">﻿</span>﻿

1667</p>

1668<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-39" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1669<h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-41" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-subheading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-subheading__text"> Anxiety in Healthy Participants Study </span> </h3>

1670<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-42" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1671Cannabidiol did not reduce responses to negative emotional stimuli or reduce anxiety in healthy participants, according to a study published in <em>Cannabis and Cannabinoid Research</em> in 2017.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="5">﻿</span>﻿ Researchers tested participants&#39; responses to negative images or words and threatening emotional faces and sensitivity to social rejection after taking oral cannabidiol.

1672</p>

1673<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-43" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1674<span class="heading-toc" id="safety"></span> <h2 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-44" class="comp mntl-sc-block health-sc-block-heading mntl-sc-block-heading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-heading__text"> Safety </span> </h2>

1675<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-45" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1676Using CBD oil may cause a number of side effects, including anxiety. Some research indicates that CBD oil may also trigger the following side effects:

1677</p>

1678<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-46" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1679<ul id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-47" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1680<li>Changes in appetite</li>

1681<li>Changes in mood</li>

1682<li><a href="https://www.verywellhealth.com/diarrhea-causes-1324505" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="1" rel="noopener noreferrer">Diarrhea</a></li>

1683<li>Dizziness</li>

1684<li>Drowsiness</li>

1685<li>Dry mouth</li>

1686<li><a href="https://www.verywellhealth.com/what-causes-low-blood-pressure-1764029" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="2" rel="noopener noreferrer">Low blood pressure</a></li>

1687<li><a href="https://www.verywellhealth.com/chronic-nausea-causes-and-treatment-4159468" data-component="link" data-source="inlineLink" data-type="internalLink" data-ordinal="3" rel="noopener noreferrer">Nausea</a></li>

1688<li>Vomiting</li>

1689</ul>

1690<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-48" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1691<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-49" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1692Cannabidiol has been found to slightly increase heart rate at a dose of 900 mg. In addition, there’s some evidence that the use of CBD oil may lead to increased levels of liver enzymes (a marker of liver damage).

1693</p>

1694<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-50" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1695<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-52" class="comp theme-takeaway mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-callout mntl-block" data-tracking-id="mntl-sc-block-callout" data-tracking-container="true">

1696<div id="mntl-sc-block-callout-body_1-0" class="comp expert-content mntl-sc-block-callout-body mntl-text-block">

1697<p>CBD oil may also interact with several medications, including benzodiazepines, calcium channel blockers, antihistamines, and some types of anti-epileptic drugs. If you are on any of these types of medications, consult your healthcare provider before using CBD oil.</p></div>

1698</div>

1699<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-53" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1700A research review found that in the treatment of certain types of refractory epilepsy, participants used lower dosages when using a CBD-rich extract compared to purified CBD products, and found adverse effects were less frequent in those using CBD-rich extracts.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="7">﻿</span>﻿

1701</p>

1702<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-54" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1703<h3 id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-56" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-subheading"> <span class="mntl-sc-block-subheading__text"> Labeling Inaccuracy </span> </h3>

1704<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-57" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1705It should also be noted that, because CBD oil is mostly unregulated, products may be incorrectly labeled. To that end, a study published in the <em>Journal of the American Medical Association</em> in 2017 found that nearly 70 percent of all CBD products sold online are mislabeled and that a number of products contain a significant amount of THC.<span class="ql-inline-citation" data-cite="6">﻿</span>﻿

1706</p>

1707<div id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-58" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-adslot mntl-block"></div>

1708<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-59" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

1709Since THC can aggravate anxiety and make your heart beat faster than normal, it’s possible that using CBD oil that contains THC might make your anxiety worse.

1710</p>

1713<p id="mntl-sc-block_1-0-62" class="comp mntl-sc-block mntl-sc-block-html">

By working with a mental health professional, you can find the anxiety treatment plan that’s right for you.

1715</p>

1718Because letting an anxiety disorder go untreated can deplete your quality of life and lead to physical health problems (such as digestive conditions), it’s crucial to consult a healthcare provider rather than self-treating. If you’re thinking of using CBD oil to help manage your anxiety (and it is legal where you live), make sure to talk with your healthcare provider about whether it&#39;s right for you.
