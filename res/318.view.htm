333<div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_1">

334<p>

335Hemp seeds are the seeds of the hemp plant, <em>

336Cannabis sativa</em>

337.</p>

338<p>

339They are from the same species as cannabis (marijuana) but a different variety.</p>

340<p>

341However, they contain only trace amounts of THC, the psychoactive compound in marijuana.</p>

342<p>

343Hemp seeds are exceptionally nutritious and rich in healthy fats, protein and various minerals.</p>

344<p>

345Here are 6 health benefits of hemp seeds that are backed up by science.</p>

358 1. Hemp Seeds Are Incredibly Nutritious</a>

359</div>

382<p>

383Technically a nut, hemp seeds are very nutritious. They have a mild, nutty flavor and are often referred to as hemp hearts.</p>

384<p>

385Hemp seeds contain over 30% fat. They are exceptionally rich in two essential fatty acids, linoleic acid (omega-6) and alpha-linolenic acid (omega-3).</p>

386<p>

387They also contain gamma-linolenic acid, which has been linked to several health benefits (<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs10681-004-4811-6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

3881</a>

389).</p>

390<p>

391Hemp seeds are a great protein source, as more than 25% of their total calories are from high-quality protein.</p>

392<p>

393That is considerably more than similar foods like <a href="/nutrition/11-proven-health-benefits-of-chia-seeds" class="content-link css-5r4717">

394chia seeds</a>

395 and <a href="/nutrition/benefits-of-flaxseeds" class="content-link css-5r4717">

396flaxseeds</a>

397, whose calories are 16–18% protein.</p>

398<p>

399Hemp seeds are also a great source of vitamin E and minerals, such as phosphorus, potassium, sodium, magnesium, sulfur, calcium, iron and zinc (<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs10681-004-4811-6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4001</a>

401, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

402<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/23088580/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4032</a>

404</hl-trusted-source>

405).</p>

406<p>

407Hemp seeds can be consumed raw, cooked or roasted. Hemp seed oil is also very healthy and has been used as a food and medicine in China for at least 3,000 years (<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs10681-004-4811-6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4081</a>

409).</p>

410<blockquote class="css-pc7ote">

411<strong>

412Summary </strong>

413Hemp seeds are rich in healthy fats and essential fatty acids. They are also a great protein source and contain high amounts of vitamin E, phosphorus, potassium, sodium, magnesium, sulfur, calcium, iron and zinc.</blockquote>

438 2. Hemp Seeds May Reduce Your Risk of Heart Disease</a>

439</div>

440</h2>

441<div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_3">

442<p>

443Heart disease is the number one cause of death worldwide (<hl-trusted-source source="World Health Organization" rationale="Highly respected international organization">

444<a href="http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs310/en/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4453</a>

446</hl-trusted-source>

447).</p>

448<p>

449Interestingly, eating hemp seeds may reduce your risk of heart disease.</p>

450<p>

451The seeds contain high amounts of the <a href="/nutrition/essential-amino-acids" class="content-link css-5r4717">

452amino acid</a>

453 arginine, which produces nitric oxide in your body (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

454<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19727602/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4554</a>

456</hl-trusted-source>

457).</p>

458<p>

459<a href="/nutrition/how-to-increase-nitric-oxide" class="content-link css-5r4717">

460Nitric oxide</a>

461 is a gas molecule that makes your blood vessels dilate and relax, leading to lowered blood pressure and a reduced risk of heart disease (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

462<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21968645" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4635</a>

464</hl-trusted-source>

465).</p>

466<p>

467In a large study in over 13,000 people, increased arginine intake corresponded with decreased levels of C-reactive protein (CRP), an inflammation marker. High levels of CRP are linked to heart disease (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

468<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15723738" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4696</a>

470</hl-trusted-source>

471, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

472<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9077376/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4737</a>

474</hl-trusted-source>

475).</p>

476<p>

477The gamma-linolenic acid found in hemp seeds has also been linked to reduced inflammation, which may decrease your risk of diseases like heart disease (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

478<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/19842026" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4798</a>

480</hl-trusted-source>

481, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

482<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/15741054/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

4839</a>

484</hl-trusted-source>

485).</p>

486<p>

487Additionally, animal studies have shown that hemp seeds or hemp seed oil may reduce blood pressure, decrease the risk of blood clot formation and help the heart recover after a heart attack (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

488<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/17122327" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

48910</a>

490</hl-trusted-source>

491, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

492<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24292743" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

49311</a>

494</hl-trusted-source>

495, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

496<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/18418423" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

49712</a>

498</hl-trusted-source>

499).</p>

500<blockquote class="css-pc7ote">

501<strong>

502Summary </strong>

503Hemp seeds are a great source of arginine and gamma-linolenic acid, which have been linked to a reduced risk of heart disease.</blockquote>

520 3. Hemp Seeds and Oil May Benefit Skin Disorders</a>

521</div>

522</h2>

523<div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_4">

524<p>

525Fatty acids may affect immune responses in your body (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

526<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/12848277" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

52713</a>

528</hl-trusted-source>

529, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

530<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10096116" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

53114</a>

532</hl-trusted-source>

533, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

534<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/10617998" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

53515</a>

536</hl-trusted-source>

537).</p>

538<p>

539Studies suggest that your immune system depends on the balance of <a href="/nutrition/optimize-omega-6-omega-3-ratio" class="content-link css-5r4717">

540omega-6 and omega-3</a>

541 fatty acids.</p>

542<p>

543Hemp seeds are a good source of polyunsaturated and essential fatty acids. They have about a 3:1 ratio of omega-6 to omega-3, which is considered in the optimal range.</p>

544<p>

545Studies have shown that giving hemp seed oil to people with eczema may improve blood levels of essential fatty acids.</p>

546<p>

547The oil may also <a href="/nutrition/12-foods-for-healthy-skin" class="content-link css-5r4717">

548relieve dry skin</a>

549, improve itchiness and reduce the need for skin medication (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

55116</a>

552</hl-trusted-source>

553, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

554<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24600196/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

55517</a>

556</hl-trusted-source>

557).</p>

558<blockquote class="css-pc7ote">

559<strong>

560Summary </strong>

561Hemp seeds are rich in healthy fats. They have a 3:1 ratio of omega-6 to omega-3, which may benefit skin diseases and provide relief from eczema and its uncomfortable symptoms.</blockquote>

586 4. Hemp Seeds Are a Great Source of Plant-Based Protein</a>

587</div>

588</h2>

589<div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_5">

590<p>

591About 25% of calories in hemp seeds come from protein, which is relatively high.</p>

592<p>

593In fact, by weight, hemp seeds provide similar amounts of protein as beef and lamb — 30 grams of hemp seeds, or 2–3 tablespoons, provide about 11 grams of protein (<a href="https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007%2Fs10681-004-4811-6" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

5941</a>

595).</p>

596<p>

597They are considered a complete protein source, which means that they provide all the essential amino acids. Your body cannot produce essential amino acids and must obtain them from your diet.</p>

598<p>

599Complete protein sources are very rare in the plant kingdom, as plants often lack the amino acid lysine. <a href="/nutrition/11-proven-benefits-of-quinoa" class="content-link css-5r4717">

600Quinoa</a>

601 is another example of a complete, <a href="/nutrition/protein-for-vegans-vegetarians" class="content-link css-5r4717">

602plant-based protein</a>

603 source.</p>

604<p>

605Hemp seeds contain significant amounts of the amino acids methionine and cysteine, as well as very high levels of arginine and glutamic acid (<a href="http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1271/bbb.62.650" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

60618</a>

607).</p>

608<p>

609The digestibility of hemp protein is also very good — better than protein from many grains, nuts and legumes (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

610<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/20977230" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

61119</a>

612</hl-trusted-source>

613).</p>

614<blockquote class="css-pc7ote">

615<strong>

616Summary </strong>

617About 25% of the calories in hemp seeds come from protein. What’s more, they contain all the essential amino acids, making them a complete protein source.</blockquote>

644 5. Hemp Seeds May Reduce Symptoms of PMS and Menopause</a>

645</div>

646</h2>

647<div class="content_body" id="TOC_TITLE_6">

648<p>

649Up to 80% of women of reproductive age may suffer from physical or emotional symptoms caused by premenstrual syndrome (PMS) (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

650<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/7556868/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

65120</a>

652</hl-trusted-source>

653).</p>

654<p>

655These symptoms are very likely caused by sensitivity to the hormone prolactin (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

656<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/24678964/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

65721</a>

658</hl-trusted-source>

659).</p>

660<p>

661Gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), found in hemp seeds, produces prostaglandin E1, which reduces the effects of prolactin (<hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

662<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/21241460" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

66322</a>

664</hl-trusted-source>

665, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

666<a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/6400557/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" class="content-link css-5r4717">

66723</a>

668</hl-trusted-source>

669, <hl-trusted-source source="PubMed Central" rationale="Highly respected database from the National Institutes of Health">

67124</a>

672</hl-trusted-source>

673).</p>

674<p>
