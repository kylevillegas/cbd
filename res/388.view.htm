1439<h2 id="bitten">

1440What Happens to Your Body After You’re Bitten by an Insect?</h2>

1441

1442<p>

1443A bite or sting from an insect can be incredibly painful, or simply uncomfortable. Depending on the type of insect, it may even lead to an infection, allergic reaction, serious illness, or death. In most cases, however, a bite or sting will cause relatively minor symptoms.</p>

1444

1445<p>

1446There are a number of insects and spiders that bite or sting. This includes ticks, mites, bedbugs, fleas, lice, horse flies, mosquitos, black flies, bees, yellow jackets, hornets, fire ants, and wasps.</p>

1447

1448<p>

1449When a bug bites or stings you, their saliva or the venom that they inject causes an immune system response. For most people, the immediate response is redness and swelling, followed by itching and soreness. Individuals who are sensitive or allergic to an insect’s venom may go into anaphylactic shock, a life-threatening condition.</p>

1450

1451<p>

1452<a href="https://medlineplus.gov/ency/article/000033.htm" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1453Common symptoms</a>

1454 of a bug bite or sting include:</p>

1457

1458<li>

1459Pain</li>

1460

1461<li>

1462Swelling</li>

1463

1464<li>

1465Itching</li>

1466

1467<li>

1468Redness</li>

1469

1470<li>

1471Burning</li>

1472

1473<li>

1474Tingling</li>

1475

1476<li>

1477Numbness</li>

1478

1479</ul>

1480

1481<p>

1482If you experience more serious symptoms that may indicate an allergic reaction, such as difficulty breathing, fainting, or chest pain, you should seek medical attention immediately.</p>

1483

1484<p>

1485Most bites and stings can be treated with simple, at-home care. This may include removing the stinger from a bee sting, washing the site, and then applying ice and/or an antihistamine cream. In addition, an oral antihistamine, pain reliever, or an anti-inflammatory medication can be taken to reduce itchiness and swelling.</p>

1487<h2 id="cbd">

1488Using CBD for Bug Bites and Stings</h2>

1489

1490<p>

1491CBD — also known as cannabidiol — is a naturally-occurring compound derived from the hemp plant (a type of cannabis). Unlike THC, another cannabinoid that is found in the cannabis plant, CBD is not psychoactive and won’t get you high. Both THC and CBD interact with the body’s <a href="https://www.greenwellnesslife.com/buyers-cbd-guide/the-science-of-cbd/">

1492endocannabinoid system (ECS)</a>

1493.</p>

1494

1495<p>

1496The ECS is responsible for maintaining homeostasis, or balance, in our bodies. It has been <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5877694/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1497linked</a>

1498 to a number of processes in our bodies, including skin and nerve function, inflammation, stress, metabolism, and mood. While scientists are still figuring out exactly how CBD interacts with the ECS, preliminary research has indicated that it may produce positive effects related to a number of conditions and symptoms.</p>

1499

1500<p>

1501As described above, bug bites and stings often cause pain and swelling due to the body’s inflammatory reaction to venom or saliva. Scientists have determined that CBD may help to reduce both <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16596786/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1502pain</a>

1503 and inflammation.</p>

1504

1506A <a href="https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fphar.2018.01259/full" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

15072018 review</a>

1508 found that CBD is effective in pain management, with no negative side effects. A 2016 study involving rats with arthritis, scientists found that applying a CBD gel <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4851925/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1509reduced pain and inflammation</a>

1510 in affected joints. Similarly, a <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27767974/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

15112016 study</a>

1512 and a <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25703248/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

15132015 review</a>

1514 found that CBD reduces inflammation in the body.</p>

1515

1516<p>

1517Research has also shown that CBD can help with a range of skin issues. In 2014, scientists determined that CBD reduces <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4151231/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1518skin inflammation</a>

1519. It may also <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2757311/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">

1520help people</a>

1521 who suffer from psoriasis, acne, dry skin, dermatitis and systemic sclerosis.</p>
