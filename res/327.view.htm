1950<h2 class="article-section__title section__title" id="d3419309e1">

1951Abstract</h2>

1952<div class="abstractSection abstractInFull">

1953<p>

1954<b>

1955Introduction:</b>

1956 Preclinical and clinical studies suggest that cannabidiol (CBD) found in Cannabis spp. has broad therapeutic value. CBD products can currently be purchased online, over the counter and at Cannabis-specific dispensaries throughout most of the country, despite the fact that CBD is generally deemed a Schedule I controlled substance by the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration and renounced as a dietary supplement ingredient by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. Consumer demand for CBD is high and growing, but few studies have examined the reasons for increasing CBD use.</p>

1957<p>

1958<b>

1959Materials and Methods:</b>

1960 A self-selected convenience sample (nâ=â2409) was recruited via an online survey designed to characterize whom, how, and why individuals are currently using CBD. The anonymous questionnaire was accessed from October 25, 2017 to January 25, 2018. Participants were recruited through social media.</p>

1961<p>

1962<b>

1963Results:</b>

1964 Almost 62% of CBD users reported using CBD to treat a medical condition. The top three medical conditions were pain, anxiety, and depression. Almost 36% of respondents reported that CBD treats their medical condition(s) âvery well by itself,â while only 4.3% reported ânot very well.â One out of every three users reported a nonserious adverse effect. The odds of using CBD to treat a medical condition were 1.44 (95% confidence interval, 1.16â1.79) times greater among nonregular users of Cannabis than among regular users.</p>

1965<p>

1966<b>

1967Conclusion:</b>

1968 Consumers are using CBD as a specific therapy for multiple diverse medical conditionsâparticularly pain, anxiety, depression, and sleep disorders. These data provide a compelling rationale for further research to better understand the therapeutic potential of CBD.</p>

1979<h2 class="article-section__title section__title" id="_i3">

1980Introduction</h2>

1981<p>

1982Cannabidiol (CBD) is one of more than a hundred cannabinoids found in <i>

1983Cannabis sativa</i>

1984 L (<i>

1985Cannabis</i>

1986 spp. or <i>

1987Cannabis</i>

1988), a plant more well known colloquially as marijuana and hemp. CBD is typically the second most abundant cannabinoid found in <i>

1989Cannabis</i>

1990 after tetrahydrocannabinol (THC).<sup>

1991<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B1" id="B1R" class="tab-link">

19921</a>

1993</sup>

1994 CBD was first isolated in 1940 and characterized structurally in 1963.<sup>

1995<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B2" id="B2R" class="tab-link">

19962</a>

1997,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B3" id="B3R" class="tab-link">

19983</a>

1999</sup>

2000</p>

2001<p>

2002CBD is well tolerated in humans and maintains a good safety profile.<sup>

2003<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B4" id="B4R" class="tab-link">

20044</a>

2005,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

20065</a>

2007</sup>

2008 Neither abuse nor dependence has been demonstrated.<sup>

2009<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

20105</a>

2011</sup>

2012 In preclinical studies, CBD shows potential therapeutic efficacy against a diverse assortment of medical conditions. These include seizure disorders, psychotic symptoms, anxiety, depression, inflammation, cancer, cardiovascular diseases, neurodegeneration, symptoms of multiple sclerosis, and chronic pain, either used alone or when coadministered with THC.<sup>

2013<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5 B6 B7 B8 B9 B10 B11 B12 B13 B14 B15 B16 B17 B18 B19 B20" id="B5 B6 B7 B8 B9 B10 B11 B12 B13 B14 B15 B16 B17 B18 B19 B20R" class="tab-link">

20145â20</a>

2015</sup>

2016</p>

2017<p>

2018In October of 2017, a New Drug Application was submitted to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) to seek approval of CBD isolated from marijuana for the treatment of two pediatric seizure disorders. Approval was granted in June, 2018, making Epidiolex (cannabidiol) the first plant-derived <i>

2019Cannabis</i>

2020 compound approved as a drug by the FDA. Availability of Epidiolex is pending Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) rescheduling of cannabidiol, which is expected to occur within 90 days.<sup>

2021<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B21 B22 B23" id="B21 B22 B23R" class="tab-link">

202221â23</a>

2023</sup>

2024 Sativex (nabiximols), a combination drug with equal parts CBD and THC extracted from marijuana, is currently approved to treat spasticity due to multiple sclerosis in &gt;30 countries worldwide but is not approved in the United States.<sup>

2025<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B17" id="B17R" class="tab-link">

202617</a>

2027,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B21" id="B21R" class="tab-link">

202821</a>

2029</sup>

2030</p>

2031<p>

2032The worldwide regulatory status of CBD is complex and constantly changing.<sup>

2033<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

20345</a>

2035</sup>

2036 While CBD is legal in many countries as a component of prescription Sativex (nabiximols), it may be simultaneously illegal as a component of a nonapproved <i>

2037Cannabis</i>

2038 extract containing &gt;0.2% (particularly in European countries) or 0.3% THC. In Europe, individual European Union Member States currently determine the legality of CBD within their borders. Most allow prescription CBD products, as do Australia and New Zealand.<sup>

2039<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

20405</a>

2041,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B24" id="B24R" class="tab-link">

204224</a>

2043</sup>

2044 Canada became the second nation in the world to legalize <i>

2045Cannabis</i>

2046 for recreational use in June 2018.<sup>

2047<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B25" id="B25R" class="tab-link">

204825</a>

2049</sup>

2050 The World Health Organization's Expert Committee on Drug Dependence recommended that CBD should not be controlled by Schedule I of the 1961 UN Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs.<sup>

2051<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

20525</a>

2053</sup>

2054 Their comprehensive report is expected this year.</p>

2055<p>

2056In the United States, until such time as it is rescheduled, CBD from marijuana is deemed by the DEA to fall within the purview of the âmarihuana extract ruleâ (Rule). A dispute over the scope of the Rule was litigated in federal court. The Court found that the Rule applies to extracts of marijuana but that the industrial hemp provisions of the 2014 Farm Act (i.e., âThe 2014 Farm Billâ) preempt the Controlled Substances Act (CSA), which the DEA enforces.<sup>

2057<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B26" id="B26R" class="tab-link">

205826</a>

2059</sup>

2060 Thus, hemp cultivated in compliance with the Farm Bill is not a controlled substance. The Court did not address the issue of CBD directly, however, and left open the issue of the legal status of CBD derived from industrial hemp, from imported ânonpsychoactive hempâ or from parts of the Cannabis sativa plant excluded from the legal definition of marijuana in the Controlled Substances Act of 1970.<sup>

2061<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B27 B28 B29 B30 B31 B32 B33 B34 B35" id="B27 B28 B29 B30 B31 B32 B33 B34 B35R" class="tab-link">

206227â35</a>

2063</sup>

2064 Despite conflicting legal interpretations, and DEA prohibition, hemp-derived CBD products can currently be purchased as dietary supplements both online and over the counter throughout most of the country. To complicate matters further, the FDA does not recognize CBD as a dietary supplement ingredient because of its status as an Investigational New Drug.<sup>

2065<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B36" id="B36R" class="tab-link">

206636</a>

2067</sup>

2068</p>

2069<p>

2070This regulatory confusion has not deterred consumers from exploring the purported benefits of CBD. Retail sales of hemp-derived CBD products in the United States reached $170 million in 2016, and are projected to grow at a 55% compound annual growth rate over the next 5 years to reach &gt;$1 billion. These estimates do not include marijuana-derived CBD.<sup>

2071<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B37" id="B37R" class="tab-link">

207237</a>

2073</sup>

2074 Although <i>

2075Cannabis</i>

2076 users have been extensively studied data characterizing the individual use of CBD are scarce. The goal of this study was to collect survey data to elucidate how, and why, individuals are using CBD.</p>

2079<h2 class="article-section__title section__title" id="_i4">

2080Methods</h2>

2081<div id="ac_i5" class="anchor-spacer">

2082</div>

2083<h3 class="article-section__title" id="_i5">

2084Survey</h3>

2085<p>

2086The study protocol was submitted electronically to the Institutional Review Board (IRB) of San Diego State University. Given the voluntary nature of the survey, and the lack of identifying information, the electronic approval process determined that no IRB approval was necessary.</p>

2087<p>

2088We developed a novel questionnaire to assess broad characteristics of self-described CBD users and underlying reasons for, and methods of, CBD use. The survey consisted of structured questions answered by either yes/no or multiple-choice responses. Questions focused on several key domains: sociodemographics; reasons for use; duration and frequency of use; method of administration; perceived clinical efficacy; and adverse effects. Study data were collected and managed using Qualtrics<sup>

2089Â®</sup>

2090 Survey Software, a secure tool allowing participants to directly enter responses anonymously.</p>

2091<p>

2092Subjects were a self-selected convenience sample who accessed the online survey from October 25, 2017 to January 25, 2018. Recruitment strategies included promotion on survey-specific Web pages on Facebook, LinkedIn, and ResearchGate. CBD product manufacturers and herbal vaporizer manufacturers assisted in recruitment by promoting links to the survey on their Facebook pages and/or via email to their customers. The only inclusion criterion was current or prior use of CBD. Respondents could skip any question(s) they did not wish to answer.</p>

2093<div id="ac_i6" class="anchor-spacer">

2094</div>

2095<h3 class="article-section__title" id="_i6">

2096Data analyses</h3>

2097<p>

2098Descriptive statistics including simple proportions were used to describe demographics, usage characteristics, medical conditions, perceived efficacy, side effects, and other CBD use preferences. Data analyses were conducted using SAS University Edition (SAS 9.4; SAS Institute Inc., Cary, NC). Univariate and bivariate comparisons were conducted using PROC FREQ and chi-square tests. 3390<h2 class="article-section__title section__title" id="_i18">

3391Discussion</h2>

3392<p>

3393To our knowledge, this is the first published survey (aside from industry reports) that specifically analyzes CBD users, as opposed to overall <i>

3394Cannabis</i>

3395 or medical <i>

3396Cannabis</i>

3397 users. The results of this study suggest that CBD is used more frequently as a specific therapy for medical conditions than for general health and well-being. This stands in contrast to the majority of marijuana users, who largely use THC-dominant <i>

3398Cannabis</i>

3399 for recreational or nonmedical reasons.<sup>

3400<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B38" id="B38R" class="tab-link">

340138</a>

3402</sup>

3403</p>

3404<p>

3405The most common medical condition for which CBD was reportedly used was pain. In preclinical studies, CBD-based analgesia is associated with potent immune-modulatory, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant activity.<sup>

3406<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B39 B40 B41 B42 B43 B44 B45 B46 B47" id="B39 B40 B41 B42 B43 B44 B45 B46 B47R" class="tab-link">

340739â47</a>

3408</sup>

3409 CBD acts as an agonist for a wide variety of cell-surface receptors including adenosine A2<sub>

3410A</sub>

3411, 5-HT<sub>

34121A</sub>

3413, TRPV1, Î±7nAch, Î±3 glycine receptors, and the peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma (PPAR-Î³) nuclear receptor. These receptors are all associated with anti-inflammatory activity.<sup>

3414<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B48 B49 B50 B51 B52 B53 B54 B55 B56 B57 B58" id="B48 B49 B50 B51 B52 B53 B54 B55 B56 B57 B58R" class="tab-link">

341548â58</a>

3416</sup>

3417 Consistent with the efficacy reported by survey respondents, CBD has been shown to reduce inflammatory cytokines in murine models of inflammatory disease and chronic and acute pain.<sup>

3418<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B59" id="B59R" class="tab-link">

341959</a>

3420</sup>

3421</p>

3422<p>

3423The endocannabinoid system may also play a role in CBD-mediated analgesia. CBD inhibits enzymes (i.e., fatty acid amide hydrolase and monoacylglycerol lipase) that degrade endocannabinoids. This inhibition is associated with increased endocannabinoid levels, analgesia, and opioid-sparing effects in preclinical models of pain.<sup>

3424<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B60" id="B60R" class="tab-link">

342560</a>

3426</sup>

3427</p>

3428<p>

3429Anxiety and depression were also commonly reported reasons for CBD use in this survey. CBD has long been proposed to inhibit THC-associated anxiety by antagonizing cannabinoid receptor activation by THC.<sup>

3430<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B61 B62 B63" id="B61 B62 B63R" class="tab-link">

343161â63</a>

3432</sup>

3433 CBD may also reduce anxiety via the serotonin 5-HT<sub>

34341A</sub>

3435 and/or GABA<sub>

3436A</sub>

3437 receptors.<sup>

3438<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B14" id="B14R" class="tab-link">

343914</a>

3440,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B64" id="B64R" class="tab-link">

344164</a>

3442</sup>

3443 These receptor pathways are being explored in hopes of novel therapeutic strategies for phobias, post-traumatic stress disorder, and drug abuse.<sup>

3444<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B65" id="B65R" class="tab-link">

344565</a>

3446,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B66" id="B66R" class="tab-link">

344766</a>

3448</sup>

3449</p>

3450<p>

3451The majority of survey respondents learned about CBD from internet research, family members, or friends. This was the case for both medical and general health and well-being users. Over 74% of respondents reported using CBD daily or more than daily. Sublingual delivery was the most common route of administration in both groups. The frequency of use of sublingual preparations found in this study contradicts a recent industry-funded CBD survey where respondents reported more frequent vaping, smoking, and topical use.<sup>

3452<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B67" id="B67R" class="tab-link">

345367</a>

3454</sup>

3455 This industry survey was collected from customers of an online medical marijuana recommendation service. Presumably, these respondents were seeking marijuana-derived products, so the frequency of inhalation as a method of administration is consistent with marijuana users overall.<sup>

3456<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B68" id="B68R" class="tab-link">

345768</a>

3458</sup>

3459 Our finding may be in part due to the fact that hemp-derived CBD products are largely distributed online and in health-food stores and widely offered as oral preparations.</p>

3460<p>

3461The percentage of respondents (55.17%) who reported regular <i>

3462Cannabis</i>

3463 use is markedly higher than national estimates. In 2015, an estimated 8.3% (â¼22.2 million people) of individuals aged 12 years or older had used marijuana in the past month.<sup>

3464<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B69" id="B69R" class="tab-link">

346569</a>

3466</sup>

3467 The reason for the higher rate of <i>

3468Cannabis</i>

3469 use among survey respondents is not clear, although one possibility is that <i>

3470Cannabis</i>

3471 users would be more likely to have heard of CBD. However, CBD use by a relatively high percentage (44.83%) of nonregular <i>

3472Cannabis</i>

3473 users suggests that individuals are not using CBD as a perceived legal route to THC consumption.</p>

3474<p>

3475Approximately half of all respondents reported using CBD for &lt;1 year. Just over 10% reported using CBD for &gt;5 years. Nonserious adverse effects were relatively common among respondents and higher among those using CBD for general health and well-being, despite the fact that this group reported less frequent use than medical users. While dry mouth, sedation/fatigue, decreased appetite, and diarrhea have previously been reported following CBD use,<sup>

3476<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B6" id="B6R" class="tab-link">

34776</a>

3478,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B7" id="B7R" class="tab-link">

34797</a>

3480</sup>

3481 other studies have demonstrated no adverse effects.<sup>

3482<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B10" id="B10R" class="tab-link">

348310</a>

3484,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B70 B71 B72" id="B70 B71 B72R" class="tab-link">

348570â72</a>

3486</sup>

3487 This dichotomy may be related to dose, interactions with prescription medications, or both. More broadly, adverse effects may also be related to the method of administration and/or the use of purified, high-dose CBD as opposed to CBD in a whole plant extract. These questions and more reinforce the need for more research on unanticipated consequences of CBD use, particularly the impact of long-term usage.<sup>

3488<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B4" id="B4R" class="tab-link">

34894</a>

3490,<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B5" id="B5R" class="tab-link">

34915</a>

3492</sup>

3493 Many of the adverse effects reported in this study (i.e., euphoria, hunger, and red eyes) are commonly associated with THC use.<sup>

3494<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B73" id="B73R" class="tab-link">

349573</a>

3496</sup>

3497 These analyses did not attempt to discriminate between hemp-derived CBD and marijuana-derived CBD products, which may have differing chemical constituents (including THC content) and therefore different effects. Further, no discrimination could be made between isolated CBD and CBD used as a constituent of a whole plant extract.</p>

3498<p>

3499Industry-originated studies have found that users are confused about the source of their CBD and the concentration of CBD and other ingredients.<sup>

3500<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B67 B68 B69 B70 B71 B72 B73 B74" id="B67 B68 B69 B70 B71 B72 B73 B74R" class="tab-link">

350167â74</a>

3502</sup>

3503 It is worth noting that independent research has confirmed that the CBD content in almost 70% of CBD-labeled products available online may be mislabeled. In one study, 43% of products were underlabeled and 26% were overlabeled for actual CBD content. More than 20% contained detectable levels of THC.<sup>

3504<a data-tab="pane-pcw-references" href="#B75" id="B75R" class="tab-link">

350575</a>

3506</sup>

3507 Since CBD-containing products are largely unregulated there is no obvious way for users to know the quantity of CBD, or other constituents, which may be present in the products they use. Given this uncertainty, it is possible that some of the reported efficacy and the adverse effects may be in part due to the inclusion of other compounds in the CBD preparation, including THC.</p>

3510<h3 class="article-section__title" id="_i19">

3511Strengths and limitations</h3>

3512<p>

3513This study has several strengths, including the size, geographic representation of the sample, wide age range of the respondents, and a focus on specific usage characteristics. In part, this was the result of utilizing multiple recruitment methodologies.</p>

3514<p>

3515In terms of limitations, the study population was a self-selected convenience sample, and as such, may not be representative of the general population or the overall population of CBD users. Individuals with favorable opinions of or experiences with CBD or <i>

3516Cannabis</i>

3517 are more likely to have responded to the questionnaire than those with negative opinions and experiences. In addition, âregular cannabis useâ was not defined in the survey and âmarijuanaâ was not distinguished from â<i>

3518Cannabis</i>

3519.â Since the survey was primarily circulated via the internet, CBD users with limited social media connectivity would be underrepresented. Finally, no mechanism for identifying repeat respondents was incorporated into the survey. Although results were examined manually, it is possible that repeat respondents may have distorted the results (i.e., Ballot stuffing).</p>

3522<h2 class="article-section__title section__title" id="_i20">

3523Conclusion</h2>

3524<p>

3525The use of CBD among individuals for both specific health conditions and general health and well-being is widespread. The results of this study demonstrate that individuals are learning about CBD from the internet, friends, or family members, rather than from healthcare professionals. CBD is being used as a specific therapy for a number of diverse medical conditionsâparticularly pain and inflammatory disorders, in addition to anxiety, depression, and sleep disorders. A large percentage of respondents indicate that CBD treats their condition(s) effectively in the absence of conventional medicine and with nonserious adverse effects. These data provide a compelling rationale for further research to better understand the therapeutic potential of CBD in treating chronic pain, anxiety, depression, sleep disorders, and other medical conditions.</p>
